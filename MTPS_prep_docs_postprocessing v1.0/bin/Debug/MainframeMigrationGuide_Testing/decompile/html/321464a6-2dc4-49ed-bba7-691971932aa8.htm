Introduction to Assessment/Discovery

Fujitsu’s Assessment/Discovery service is intended to enable enterprises to address problems raised by their mainframe environments—for example, high maintenance costs, demands from users and customers for improved service, and limited mainframe tools and capabilities. Its Assessment/Discovery service recommends methodologies and architectures to support the management, development, and production paradigms in the new environment.

Fujitsu offers two types of Assessment/Discovery services:

  • Rapid Assessment. This is a two-week interview and investigation process to help you decide whether you should undertake a migration project. If you decide to migrate, Rapid Assessment will recommend the most appropriate modernization option.

  • QuickStart Delivery Verification (QuickStart). QuickStart is an eight-week process aimed at enterprises that have decided in favor of migration. It is a focused yet streamlined process that assesses in detail the mainframe environment and conducts a proof-of-concept conversion on a subset of the customer’s application. The QuickStart output also includes pricing for a complete migration project.

Fujitsu can combine its onshore and offshore resources to accommodate the specific needs of its clients.

Approach to Assessment/Discovery

Rapid Assessment and QuickStart are not mutually exclusive services. Rapid Assessment can help you to adopt a plan and schedule to fit into your organizational structure and budgetary constraints. Thus, it can serve as a preliminary phase that leads to the more detailed QuickStart assessment process.

The following key elements in Fujitsu’s approach to Assessment/Discovery are common to both Rapid Assessment and QuickStart:

  • Project planning. Fujitsu uses templates from its Macroscope Productivity Center to develop a project plan that defines the scope, deliverables, and schedule of the Assessment/Discovery project.

  • Joint effort. The Assessment/Discovery project is a joint effort between Fujitsu and your organization. The joint effort ensures that appropriate procedures are being followed and that the proper resources are being utilized for efficiency and completeness of the project.

  • Documentation. Fujitsu uses the Macroscope methodology to document the Assessment/Discovery project.

  • Deliverables. Both Rapid Assessment and QuickStart define the deliverables that you should expect to receive.

  • Application source. In order to make efficient use of project time, Fujitsu requires that you develop an inventory of your application source and deliver it to them—on a CD/DVD or via FTP transmission—so that they can load it into their EZSource analysis engine.

  • Questionnaire. Both Rapid Assessment and QuickStart initiate the Assessment/Discovery project with a comprehensive questionnaire that builds knowledge of the mainframe environment.

How Assessment/Discovery Works

As previously mentioned, Fujitsu offers two types of Assessment/Discovery services: the shorter Rapid Assessment and the longer QuickStart. This section describes each of these options in greater detail.

Rapid Assessment

The purpose of Rapid Assessment is to evaluate the mainframe environment and draw conclusions that can help you decide whether you should undertake a migration project. Rapid Assessment evaluates modernization options and recommends which option is the most suitable for you. It addresses problems with the mainframe environment that you would like to resolve—for example, the inability of the infrastructure to accommodate the demands placed upon its resources because of increases in customer, business, or market demands for services from the mainframe applications.

Time Frame

Rapid Assessment is a short process lasting two weeks. It begins with an on-site review of the mainframe environment. The Fujitsu project team collects the application source from your project team and performs an inventory, environment, complexity, and risk analysis of this application source.

Modernization Options

Rapid Assessment evaluates the following five modernization options:

  • Keep the current systems. The organization decides against migration in favor of retaining its mainframe environment.

  • Re-host. This option consists of porting an application from a mainframe to industry-standard servers. Re-hosting preserves the organization’s investment in code, business logic, and associated staff skills.

  • Re-engineer. Re-engineering typically involves a two-pronged approach: building a front-end that can extend the capabilities of the legacy system, and changing from a mainframe to a Service-Oriented Architecture (SOA) based on Microsoft .NET Framework.

  • Consolidate. Consolidation enables you to eliminate duplication by moving data and functionality to a smaller number of systems.

  • Replace. This option offers two main approaches. In the first approach, the legacy application is replaced while the underlying mainframe is kept. In the second approach, both the legacy application and the underlying mainframe are replaced.

QuickStart

QuickStart is a pilot project for the subsequent full-scale migration project. Like Rapid Assessment, it produces modernization recommendations, but it goes beyond Rapid Assessment by testing and verifying these recommendations.

At the end of the project, QuickStart produces a report that describes the operational functions, application architecture, and technology infrastructure required by the recommended modernization option. The results produced by QuickStart provide the basis for estimating the time-and-materials pricing or fixed pricing for the subsequent full migration effort.

QuickStart consists of two main phases (see Figure 5.1):

  • QuickPlan. This phase analyzes the environment from the perspective of migration. It produces the roadmap for the subsequent conversion phase. Your project team selects a subset—QuickSlice—of an application to be used in the QuickProof phase.

  • QuickProof. This is a proof-of-concept phase in which a subset of an application is converted to the target platform to demonstrate that the recommended modernization strategy is feasible. This phase also scrutinizes your environment and creates a high-level plan that includes conversion estimates, tool replacement mapping, training recommendations, and hardware strategies.

Preparing for QuickStart

Your project team is expected to review potential QuickSlice subsets and select one to be converted to the target platform in the QuickProof phase. Given the accelerated nature of the QuickStart process, your project team is expected to assemble the following items prior to the beginning of QuickStart:

  • Inventory of applications—organized by source type such as COBOL, JCL, and REXX—delivered to the Fujitsu project team on CD/DVD media or via FTP transmission.

  • Execution scripts, run sheets, and relevant documentation that will be used to test the converted batch and/or online systems in the target environment.

  • QuickSlice test data, along with the scripts to create it, to be used in the QuickProof phase to test the converted application.

Fujitsu’s Data Base Administrator (DBA) loads this test data into a Microsoft SQL Server™ database in the target environment.

Phase 1: QuickPlan

QuickPlan is the first phase of QuickStart. It is a two-week process intended to initiate the project, complete the questionnaire, select a subset of the applications for conversion testing, and produce a plan for the QuickProof phase of QuickStart.

Questionnaire

The first step in the QuickPlan phase is to complete the questionnaire. The questionnaire is a critical part of the assessment process because it captures important details about the products, practices, personnel, and concerns associated with the mainframe environment. Each section of the questionnaire starts a new page to make it easier for you to distribute it to your appropriate staff to answer the questions in the section. The questionnaire is a valuable guide for developing the migration plan and budget estimates.

For more information about QuickStart or to obtain a copy of the questionnaire, contact Fujitsu.

QuickSlice

The next step in the QuickPlan phase is to identify a QuickSlice. QuickSlice is a subset, or “slice,” of an application that will be used in the QuickProof as a pilot conversion test. Your Assessment/Discovery team will select the candidate application from which the subset or slice will be made. Like the questionnaire, the QuickSlice forms a critical part of the QuickPlan process because it must be representative of the applications and their processing in the mainframe environment, and it must address the migration issues that you expect the Assessment/Discovery project to resolve. The slice should be such that it can be tested within the six-week time frame of the QuickProof phase. Fujitsu will analyze the QuickSlice application and identify any scope issues.

QuickKick

Although the questionnaire and QuickSlice belong to the QuickPlan phase, in practice they will have been completed by you before the Fujitsu team arrives at your site. The project starts with a kickoff meeting called QuickKick. The purpose of the meeting is to accomplish the following tasks:

  • Introduce the two teams (your organization and Fujitsu), including the subject matter experts in your team.

  • Review the questionnaire.

  • Confirm the project’s scope.

  • Review the QuickSlice.

  • Identify migration concerns.

QuickPlan Sign-Off

Fujitsu will complete the QuickPlan roadmap that defines the conversion assignments and scheduling. The QuickProof phase begins after your project sponsors sign off on the QuickPlan document.

Phase 2: QuickProof

QuickProof is the second phase of QuickStart. This phase takes the QuickSlice defined in QuickPlan and migrates it to the .NET environment to verify that migration of the legacy applications is feasible. QuickProof consists of two main tasks:

  • Understanding the current environment (QuickDive).

  • Converting QuickSlice to the new platform (QuickFix).

QuickDive: Analysis and Estimation Track

The project team focuses on acquiring an understanding of the customer’s development and implementation methodology, application inventory, processing architecture, and operational model. It investigates any issues that the questionnaire may have raised, and it interviews the appropriate subject matter experts to resolve these issues.

QuickDive uses the EZSource analysis engine and a customized migration toolkit to identify conversion exceptions—parts of QuickSlice that Fujitsu’s NeoKicks, NeoBatch, and NetCOBOL tools are not designed to handle. EZSource will identify coding issues, dependencies, missing modules, and complexities that must be resolved during the migration process.

Mapping Mainframe Components to Their .NET Counterparts

Platform migration is a modernization strategy in which various mainframe processes and tools—administration, development, testing, operations, and production—are migrated to the new platform. In such cases, it is necessary to determine how these processes and tools will be mapped to their counterpart processes and tools in the new environment.

QuickDive assesses all application inventories and maps them to their respective .NET counterparts. However, the QuickProof conversion will test an application subset that has been carefully chosen to represent normal processing within the mainframe environment. QuickFix: Conversion Track

The second task of QuickProof is to demonstrate that the QuickSlice application can be successfully converted as a functioning .NET application. QuickFix takes the QuickSlice—typically consisting of a mix of online transaction and batch applications—and converts it to operate in the .NET environment. It also migrates the data and execution tools to their corresponding targets in the .NET environment—for example, test data to a computer running SQL Server, VSAM files to Btrieve and the NetCOBOL.NET file system, BMS maps to ASP.NET interfaces, and JCL to Jscripts. The converted application will use NeoKicks online transaction processing and NeoBatch for batch processing.You will be able to interact with the converted applications on a laptop and via the Internet.

The QuickProof approach to conversion and testing has two main advantages:

  • It offers a powerful and flexible development environment for developers. It also enables Fujitsu to perform QuickProof conversions via remote access and to use offshore resources that reduce costs for you.

  • It improves access to the applications for your customers and internal users. QuickProof can also be used to test the throughput performance of the converted applications by deploying them on different hardware configurations to determine the optimal production performance.

Fujitsu uses QuickScope, a customized subset of the Fujitsu Macroscope methodology, to define all aspects of the project. The project uses specially formulated plans for:

  • QuickSlice details

  • Human resources

  • Communications process and schedule

  • Project statement or overall definition

QuickScope also incorporates formal reporting for:

  • Resource requests

  • Change requests

  • Status reports

  • Issues log

  • Deliverable review

To ensure that the project team accomplishes the goal of the project within the eight-week period and to expedite the master migration project, Fujitsu uses their analysis and development software suite including:

  • EZSource. For parsing the code and analyzing an application’s completeness, flow of logic, and complexity.

  • NetCOBOL. .NET. Extensive COBOL support within the .NET Framework, with full access to all .NET functionality.

  • NeoKicks. Transaction support for migrating CICS applications.

  • NeoBatch. Batch support for migrating JCL, procedures, mainframe utilities, SORT support, and so on.

The wizards in the Fujitsu suite automatically convert many facets of the mainframe application to .NET constructs. These rapid conversion tools help to decrease the overall project timeline, thereby decreasing project expense.

Resources

Because Fujitsu recognizes that no one is more knowledgeable about your environment than your personnel, QuickStart was conceived as a joint Fujitsu-Customer project Therefore, your participation in the project is critical to its success. Your executive sponsor will co-chair the steering committee along with Fujitsu’s executive sponsor.

The steering committee consists of the executive sponsors, your project manager, and Fujitsu’s QuickStart project manager. The steering committee drives all project efforts and resolves all project issues. It is important that the steering committee co-chairs attend all status meetings; they may also need to attend impromptu meetings to address specific issues that might arise during the project.

Fujitsu recommends that your project team include subject matter experts (SMEs) to ensure that the team addresses all the issues promptly and appropriately.

Procedural Assumptions

Fujitsu uses its proprietary Macroscope procedures and templates to manage project change control and other technical activities. However, Fujitsu can readily accommodate its methodology to the document formats used in your organization.

Pricing

QuickStart can be performed either on-site at your facility or off-site at a Fujitsu facility. The off-site option greatly reduces travel expenses for you, and it also enables the QuickStart team to increase its productivity because the team will be able to draw on its normal support structure at its own site. If you choose the off-site option, Fujitsu’s Project Managers and Solutions Architects will travel to your site at regular intervals to interview your SMEs, review status reports, and meet with the project steering committee. The off-site option greatly reduces the price of QuickStart resources and travel and, most importantly, the cost to you.

Risk Factors

A migration project, or even a stand-alone assessment project to evaluate the issues and cost for a migration project, is not a small undertaking. Your project team and Fujitsu’s QuickStart team must work closely together to achieve your defined goals. Your project team provides guidance and knowledge about the mainframe environment. Fujitsu provides the processes to document issues, estimate costs, define replacement mapping strategy, and develop the migration plan. The following factors are critical to the success of the project:

  • Accuracy of the inventory information and scope of work provided by you.

  • Access and availability of your key personnel and their interaction with Fujitsu staff so as not to create any undue delays.

  • Timely access to resources and documentation describing the key interfaces, scripts, and functionality of your mainframe applications.
